Use rmdir if you want to remove just the directory symbolic link (by itself.)

When you begin to live with (make use of) symblic links in windows. You may have to remove some symbolic links for maintainence.

It feels all okay until symbolic links whose target are directories, those ones created by /d parameters by `mklink` tool, comes into play.

Don't use the del command, as it will recursivly delete all the files under the target directory.

Use rmdir to delete just the symbolic link itself.
